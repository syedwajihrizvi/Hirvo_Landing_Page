OAuth Guide

Effective Date: April 4, 2026

Hirvo uses OAuth 2.0 for secure authentication and authorization when integrating with third-party services. This guide provides an overview of how OAuth works within the Hirvo app and how to set it up for your account. OAuth allows users to grant Hirvo access to their information on other services (like Google, Zoom, Microsoft, Dropbox, and Webex) without sharing their passwords. It is a state-of-the-art protocol that ensures secure and efficient authorization, enabling users to seamlessly connect their accounts and enhance their experience on Hirvo.

OAuth Services Used

We utilize the following OAuth services to enhance user experience. These services are optional and require user consent.

a. Google

  • User logs into their google account via the app and gives us consent
  • For business users, they can directly create meetings and manage schedules
  • For non-business users, they can upload files directly from Google Drive such as resumes, cover letters, etc
  • We lose consent once the token expires or the user logs out

b. Zoom

  • User logs into their Zoom account via the app and gives us consent
  • For business users, they can directly create meetings and manage schedules
  • We lose consent once the token expires or the user logs out

c. Microsoft

  • User logs into their MS account via the app and gives us consent
  • For business users, they can directly create meetings and manage schedules
  • For non-business users, they can upload files directly from OneDrive such as resumes, cover letters, etc
  • We lose consent once the token expires or the user logs out

d. Dropbox

  • User logs into their Dropbox account via the app and gives us consent
  • For non-business users, they can upload files directly from Dropbox such as resumes, cover letters, etc
  • We lose consent once the token expires or the user logs out

d. Webex

  • User logs into their Webex account via the app and gives us consent
  • For business users, they can directly create meetings and manage schedules
  • We lose consent once the token expires or the user logs out
